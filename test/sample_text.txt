The morning sun cast long golden shadows across the empty classroom. Rows of wooden desks sat waiting for students who had not yet arrived. On the chalkboard, half-erased equations from yesterday's lesson remained like pale ghosts of forgotten knowledge. A single potted plant on the windowsill stretched its leaves toward the light, patient and green.

Outside, birds darted between the branches of an old oak tree, singing in short bursts before falling silent again. Somewhere down the hallway, a door opened and closed. Footsteps echoed briefly, then faded. The clock on the wall ticked steadily, indifferent to the stillness of the room.

A crumpled piece of paper lay on the floor near the third row. It had been there since Tuesday, missed by the janitor's broom. No one had bothered to pick it up. Soon the bell would ring and the room would fill with noise, movement, and life.
